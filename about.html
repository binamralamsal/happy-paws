About the rescue

We help pets get from scared and stray to safe and loved.

Happy Paws is a local animal rescue organisation. We are not a big fancy shelter, just a practical group of foster carers, volunteers, and animal people doing the work.

Our purpose

Why Happy Paws exists

The organisation began in 2018 after a few volunteers started feeding and rescuing animals from streets, car parks, and abandoned properties. At first it was only a weekend thing, but there were always more animals needing help.

Now we rescue homeless cats and dogs, arrange vet care, place them with foster families, and help them find permanent homes. Adoption matters because it gives animals a second chance and also opens a foster space for the next animal who needs help.

We try to be honest with adopters. Some pets are easy. Some need training, quiet homes, or extra time. A good match is better than a quick adoption that falls apart later.

What we do

Our rescue work

Rescue and foster care

Animals stay in foster homes where they can recover and learn normal routines again.

Vet support

Pets are checked by a vet, vaccinated, microchipped, and desexed before adoption.

Careful matching

We talk with adopters, answer questions, and try to match animals to homes that suit them.

Community education

We encourage responsible pet ownership, desexing, and kinder treatment of local animals.

Why adoption is important

Adopting helps more than one animal.

When someone adopts, the pet gets a proper home and our foster network gets space to help another animal. It also reduces demand for careless breeding and helps the community see rescue pets as normal family animals, not "problem pets".

  • Adoption gives homeless animals a real chance.
  • Most rescue pets are already vet checked.
  • Foster carers can explain the pet's personality.
  • You are supporting a local community service.
