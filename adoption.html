Adoption information

What to expect before adopting a pet

We want adoption to feel exciting, but also realistic. Pets need time, money, care, and a safe space.

01

Choose a pet and send an enquiry

Read the available pet profiles and contact us with the name of the animal you are interested in. It helps to tell us about your household, other pets, and daily routine.

02

Talk with our adoption volunteer

We ask a few questions to check the match. This is not meant to be scary, it just protects the pet and helps avoid returns later.

03

Meet and greet

You meet the pet before deciding. For dogs, existing dogs in the home may need to meet too. For cats, we talk about indoor safety and settling in.

04

Home check and adoption agreement

We check basic safety such as fencing, hazards, and whether the landlord allows pets. Then we complete a simple adoption agreement.

05

Bring them home

The first days can be messy or nervous. We give settling tips and remain available if you need help after adoption.

Before you apply

Things to prepare

Food and supplies

Prepare food, bowls, bedding, toys, litter, leash, or crate depending on the pet.

Safe space

Set up a quiet area so the pet can decompress in the first few days.

Ongoing costs

Plan for food, vet visits, grooming, registration, and emergency expenses.

Time

New pets need attention, training, and patience. They do not understand your schedule straight away.

Conditions

Basic adoption conditions

These conditions help keep animals safe after adoption. They are simple, but important.

  • Adopters must provide accurate contact details.
  • Rental homes need permission for pets.
  • Dogs need safe fencing or a clear exercise plan.
  • Cats should be kept indoors or in a secure outdoor enclosure.
  • If the adoption does not work out, the pet must be returned to Happy Paws.
